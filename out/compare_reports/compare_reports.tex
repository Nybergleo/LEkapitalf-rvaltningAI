\section*{A. Common Themes Across Reports}
\subsection*{Operational trends}
\subsubsection*{Restructuring, efficiency measures and rightsizing}
Description: Companies report targeted measures to reduce costs, restructuring charges and ongoing rightsizing to strengthen operational efficiency and long-term competitiveness.

Companies: Alleima; Atlas Copco; Sandvik; SKF; Trelleborg; Volvo

Tone: mixed

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "initiating a number of targeted measures ... cost savings of just over SEK 200 million per year"
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "included an MSEK -152 restructuring cost in the Vacuum Technique business"
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "IAC of SEK -643 million, comprising of structural measures within Machining"
  \item Evidence: SKF: "rightsizing initiatives continues at high pace"
  \item Evidence: Trelleborg: "Items affecting comparability ... restructuring costs"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "Adjustments ... Transformation to zero-emission vehicles"
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Mergers, acquisitions and divestments}
Description: Many reports highlight active M\&A and bolt-on acquisitions and some divestments as strategic moves to strengthen portfolios and target growth segments.

Companies: Atlas Copco; Sandvik; Trelleborg; Volvo

Tone: positive

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "The following acquisitions were closed in the quarter" and long list of deals
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "completed ten acquisitions" and list of 2025 acquisitions
  \item Evidence: Trelleborg: "acquisition of Masterseals ... finalized"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "divestment of its ownership stake in SDLG ... positive effect of SEK 811 M"
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Investments, capacity and production adjustments}
Description: Companies report capex increases, capacity optimization, plant openings or temporary production adjustments to match market and strategic needs.

Companies: Alleima; Atlas Copco; Sandvik; SKF; Trelleborg; Volvo

Tone: mixed

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "increased investments" and capex SEK -289 in quarter
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "Net investments in rental equipment were MSEK -487 ... property, plant and equipment MSEK -851"
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "Investments in tangible and intangible assets (capex) amounted to SEK 0.8 billion"
  \item Evidence: SKF: "new global Super-precision bearing centre in Italy"
  \item Evidence: Trelleborg: "factory inaugurations are planned in Vietnam and Costa Rica"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "production rates being raised before summer ... production adjustments"
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{External factors}
\subsubsection*{Currency headwinds and translation effects}
Description: All reports highlight significant negative impacts from exchange rate movements on revenues or operating income.

Companies: Alleima; Atlas Copco; Sandvik; SKF; Trelleborg; Volvo

Tone: negative

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "currency effects of SEK -41 million" on adjusted EBIT
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "Changes in exchange rates had a negative effect of MSEK 1 695" YTD
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "Exchange rate had a negative impact of SEK 218 million"
  \item Evidence: SKF: "currency headwind ... negative SEK 90 m on EBITA"
  \item Evidence: Trelleborg: "The exchange rate effect ... had a negative impact of SEK 189 m on EBITA"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "currency movements had a negative impact of SEK 1,626 M"
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Trade policy, tariffs and tariffs mitigation}
Description: Several companies discuss trade policy turbulence, tariffs and steps taken to mitigate impacts (surcharges, rerouting, price actions).

Companies: Alleima; Atlas Copco; Sandvik; SKF; Volvo

Tone: mixed

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "trade policy turbulence" noted in market conditions
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "increased costs related to trade tariffs... mitigated" and "tariff levels"
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "Tariff surcharges were implemented ... to mitigate current tariff levels"
  \item Evidence: SKF: "Section 232 Tariffs ... we expect in Q4 to largely compensate"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "net tariff impact was negative ... expected to be approximately SEK 1 billion in Q4"
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Raw material and metal price effects}
Description: Metal and alloy price effects and raw material cost volatility are called out as impacts on gross margin and adjusted results.

Companies: Alleima; Sandvik; Volvo

Tone: negative

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "metal price effects had an impact of SEK -70 million"
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "global trade restrictions on tungsten powder ... led to stricter supplies and significant price increases" (positive effect for Sandvik)
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "increased material ... costs" negatively affected adjusted operating income
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Demand and order-related commentary}
\subsubsection*{Regional demand differences and customer caution}
Description: Multiple reports describe mixed demand across regions: weakness in Europe or North America for some businesses, strength in Asia/Medical/Mining for others; a cautious customer stance is often noted.

Companies: Alleima; Atlas Copco; Sandvik; SKF; Trelleborg; Volvo

Tone: mixed

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "weakness in Europe and North America ... cautious attitude among customers, especially in Europe"
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "demand was mixed ... increased in the Americas and Europe, basically unchanged in Asia"
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "Demand in Mining remained very strong ... Infrastructure market remained subdued"
  \item Evidence: SKF: "Industrial business grew 4\% ... Automotive declined -2\%"
  \item Evidence: Trelleborg: "Industrial business grew 4\% ... Automotive declined -2\%"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "European truck market replacement-driven ... North America long-haul freight market remains in recession"
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Service and aftermarket resilience}
Description: Service businesses are highlighted as a source of stability and growth, supporting margins and cash generation.

Companies: Atlas Copco; Sandvik; Volvo; Alleima; SKF

Tone: positive

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "demand for service ... order intake increased in all regions"
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "The aftermarket business held up well ... Parts and Services reached an all-time high"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "service sales growing by 4\%"
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "Free operating cash flow ... impacted by ... increased investments" while service noted as resilient
  \item Evidence: SKF: "Service sales increased by 4\% adjusted for currency"
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Order backlog and book-to-bill mentions}
Description: Reports reference order intake, rolling 12-month metrics, book-to-bill, and backlog visibility in key segments.

Companies: Alleima; Atlas Copco; Sandvik; Volvo

Tone: neutral

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "Order intake for the rolling 12-month period amounted to SEK 18,665" and "Book-to-bill was 97\%"
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "Orders received ... organically unchanged" and geographic breakdown
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "Book-to-bill of 105\%" and detailed order intake by division
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "net order intake for heavy-duty trucks declined by 15\%" and backlog commentary
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Cost or margin-related commentary}
\subsubsection*{Price, mix and pricing actions}
Description: Companies report price and mix execution as a key driver offsetting cost inflation and supporting margins.

Companies: Alleima; Sandvik; Atlas Copco; SKF; Volvo; Trelleborg

Tone: positive

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "price leadership" and "price management" references
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "Good price execution and savings"
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "margin was positively affected by currency, while increased costs related to trade tariffs and dilution from acquisitions affected the margin negatively"
  \item Evidence: SKF: "price and mix contribution"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "good price realization"
  \item Evidence: Trelleborg: "price/mix contribution, driven by pricing activities and portfolio management"
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Inflation, labor costs and manufacturing underabsorption}
Description: Reports highlight wage inflation, underabsorption and volume-related inefficiencies as pressure points on margins.

Companies: Alleima; Atlas Copco; Sandvik; SKF; Volvo

Tone: negative

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "impacted by a weaker Europe and the extended maintenance stoppage... underabsorption"
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "lower revenue volumes and increased costs related to trade tariffs affected the margin negatively"
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "wage inflation, volume related cost inefficiencies and tariffs"
  \item Evidence: SKF: "lower manufacturing volumes as well as a significant currency headwind"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "increased material, manufacturing and tariff costs"
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Adjusted results and items affecting comparability (IAC)}
Description: Several companies present adjusted operating profit metrics and disclose items affecting comparability such as divestments, impairments and restructuring.

Companies: Alleima; Atlas Copco; Sandvik; SKF; Trelleborg; Volvo

Tone: neutral

\begin{itemize}
  \item Evidence: Alleima: "Adjusted operating profit (EBIT) excludes items affecting comparability"
  \item Evidence: Atlas Copco: "Adjusted operating profit decreased 6\% ... Adjusted operating profit was MSEK 8 862"
  \item Evidence: Sandvik: "Items affecting comparability amounted to SEK 63 million (-455) in Q3 2025"
  \item Evidence: SKF: "Items affecting comparability were high in the quarter at approximately MSEK 750"
  \item Evidence: Trelleborg: "Items affecting comparability for the quarter totaled SEK -72 m"
  \item Evidence: Volvo: "In Q3 2025, a positive effect of SEK 811 M was excluded from adjusted operating income"
\end{itemize}

\section*{B. Notable Differences}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Sandvik shows sector-specific strong growth (Mining, BEV) whereas Volvo highlights truck market softness in North America; Sandvik emphasizes record orders in Mining while Volvo reports declining truck orders versus a year ago
  \item Volvo reports a large one-off positive effect from divesting SDLG (SEK 811 M) whereas several peers report multiple acquisitions as growth strategy (Atlas Copco, Sandvik)
  \item Atlas Copco emphasizes very strong operating cash flow and large M\&A list; Volvo reports significant net financial position and detailed leverage metrics
  \item SKF and Trelleborg place explicit emphasis on sustainability milestones (SBTi, EcoVadis, CO2 targets), while others mention sustainability but with less prominence in these quarters
  \item Company-specific operational events differ: Alleima reports an extended maintenance stoppage impacting earnings; Volvo and Sandvik report product launches and safety ratings (Euro NCAP) â€” unique operational impacts per company
\end{itemize}
