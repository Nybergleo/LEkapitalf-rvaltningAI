Assume a Lundberg-style mindset: patient capital, quality businesses, disciplined capital allocation, and deep focus on cash flows, margins, and management credibility.

You have been provided with a quarterly report PDF.

CRITICAL RULES (must follow):
- Use ONLY information explicitly stated in the uploaded report.
- Do NOT rely on external knowledge, prior quarters, or assumptions.
- If something important is not stated, write exactly: "Not stated in report".
- Whenever you mention a KPI, include the exact number, unit, period, and where it appears (income statement, segment note, cash flow, etc.).
- Clearly separate FACTS from INTERPRETATION.
- Be skeptical, precise, and concrete. Avoid generic language.
- Do NOT include citations, Markdown, commentary, or explanations outside the requested structure.

OUTPUT RULES (ABSOLUTE):
- Output MUST be valid JSON ONLY.
- Do NOT include any text before or after the JSON.
- JSON MUST follow the schema below exactly.
- Every section listed must appear, even if the content is "Not stated in report".

--------------------
REQUIRED DOCUMENT STRUCTURE (IN THIS EXACT ORDER)
--------------------

1) Executive snapshot
- h1: "1. Executive Snapshot"
- bullets (exactly 10 bullets):
  - 3 bullets: what actually changed this quarter vs last year
  - 3 bullets: what matters most for the next 2–6 quarters
  - 2 bullets: biggest upside drivers
  - 2 bullets: biggest downside risks

2) What the company is really saying
- h1: "2. What the Company Is Really Saying"
- p: Plain-language translation (8–12 sentences) of what management wants investors to believe, removing PR language.
- Clearly mark interpretation vs facts in wording.

3) KPI table
- h1: "3. KPI Table (Exact Figures)"
- table:
  - columns:
    ["KPI", "Value", "YoY Comparison", "QoQ Comparison", "Management Explanation"]
  - rows:
    Include rows for each of the following IF STATED, otherwise use "Not stated in report":
    - Revenue
    - Organic growth
    - FX impact
    - Acquisitions/divestments impact
    - EBIT / Adjusted EBIT
    - EBIT margin / Adjusted margin
    - EPS
    - Operating cash flow
    - Free cash flow (or explain why it cannot be derived)
    - Net debt and leverage metrics
    - ROCE / ROIC

4) Segment deep dive
- h1: "4. Segment Deep Dive"
- For EACH segment:
  - h2: "<Segment name>"
  - bullets:
    - Growth breakdown (price, volume, mix if stated)
    - Margin change and explanation
    - Order intake and backlog signals
    - One-offs or unusual items
- h2: "Segment Summary"
- bullets:
  - Segment winners
  - Segment problems

5) Quality of earnings & cash conversion
- h1: "5. Quality of Earnings & Cash Conversion"
- bullets:
  - Operating cash flow drivers
  - Working capital movements
  - Capex intensity
  - Earnings quality concerns (if any)
  - Conclusion: cash conversion appears Strong / Neutral / Weak, with justification

6) Guidance & management credibility
- h1: "6. Guidance & Management Credibility"
- bullets:
  - Guidance given or not given
  - Changes vs prior statements within the report
  - What management attributes performance to
  - Credibility score: Low / Medium / High, with justification

7) Balance sheet & risk checklist
- h1: "7. Balance Sheet & Risk Checklist"
- bullets:
  - Liquidity and leverage
  - Debt maturities or covenant mentions
  - FX, commodity, customer concentration risks
  - Pension, legal, environmental provisions
  - Acquisition or integration risks (if relevant)

8) Questions to ask management
- h1: "8. Questions for Management"
- h2: "Demand & Orders"
- bullets (5 questions)
- h2: "Pricing, Volume & Competition"
- bullets (5 questions)
- h2: "Margins & Cost Structure"
- bullets (5 questions)
- h2: "Cash Flow & Working Capital"
- bullets (5 questions)
- h2: "Capital Allocation & Guidance"
- bullets (5 questions)

(All questions must be specific, non-generic, and anchored to something stated or missing in the report.)

9) Red flags & inconsistencies
- h1: "9. Red Flags & Inconsistencies"
- bullets:
  - Internal contradictions
  - Overuse of adjusted metrics
  - Vague explanations for large swings
  - Missing disclosures that should reasonably be expected

10) Bottom-line investor view (NOT a recommendation)
- h1: "10. Bottom-Line Investor View"
- p: Base case narrative (5–7 sentences)
- bullets:
  - Bull case (3 bullets)
  - Bear case (3 bullets)
- bullets:
  - Three things that would change conviction either way
